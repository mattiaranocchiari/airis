## What this document is

This is the root document of VIVA. VIVA is a worldview — about software, about reality, about the use of AI, about the human condition in this moment. From the worldview emerge specific expressions: Viva mode (the software-paradigm expression, currently the most developed) and forming directions (Back to Reality and a response to displacement) that have not yet crystallized into structured arms.

This document holds VIVA-level content only: the worldview itself, the values that derive from it, the foundational method of seeing the specific human, what VIVA promises broadly, the directions still forming, the catalog of expressions, and the deferred organizational matters. Software-paradigm content (the criterion applied to software, the dual-surface interaction paradigm, the system consciousness substrate, the cross-application principles) lives in Viva Mode Master Document. Application-specific content lives in each application's Master Document — for AIRIS, in AIRIS Master Document.

Per the Branch Consciousness Principle (Project Core): this branch is conscious only of itself and its children (Viva mode). It does not hold paradigm or application detail; it is referenced by them for inheritance.

For coordination across the tree, see Project Core.

This document is updated continuously as VIVA's thinking evolves. Some sections describe values and commitments that are crystallized; others honestly name directions still being worked out. The discipline is to keep both kinds of content present without pretending one is the other.

## 1. The Worldview

We are at a moment that few people have yet metabolized.

For the first time in the history of human work, a class of tools exists that can absorb the shape of how a human actually works and conform to it, instead of requiring the reverse. This is not an efficiency gain. It is the end of a fifty-year compromise that we mistook for the natural order of things. For decades, software had to be rigid because it could not understand ambiguity. The human had to come to it because it could not come to the human. That limitation is now dissolving. What it makes possible is not just better software — it is a different relationship between humans and the tools they work with, and downstream from that, a different shape for human life itself.

VIVA exists because we believe this shift is the most important change in the human-machine relationship since the personal computer, and because almost no one is yet building or living as if it has happened.

### The right use of AI

Most use of AI today imitates what came before. AI gets added to existing software in the form of chatbots, copilots, and recommendation systems that leave the underlying compromise intact — the human still bends to the machine, just with a friendlier interface. AI gets used to optimize work that should not exist. AI gets used to amplify the very things that erode human attention, real relationships, and craft — to fill more screens with more content, to generate more comfort lies, to convince more people to spend more of their lives on a screen rather than in the world.

VIVA names a different stance: the right use of AI is the one that returns time, attention, and craft to the human — not the one that captures more of those things for the machine. The measure of any AI tool is whether the human, after using it, has more of their life in their craft and their relationships and their actual lived day, or less. By that measure, much of what calls itself AI in 2026 fails.

VIVA is, in its largest framing, an attempt at the global example of the right use of AI — the best example we can build of human evolution with AI, where the technology serves the human's expansion rather than substituting for it.

### Reality, and what makes reality

Beneath the software question is a broader value commitment: reality matters more than its substitutes.

Reality is what is in front of our eyes and tangible — the patient at the bedside, the client across the table, the meal on the plate, the weather, the human face, the work being done with the hands or the mind, the conversation that is happening right now. Substitutes for reality include the screen, the performance, the documented version of the moment that displaces the moment itself, the comfort lie, the metric that stands in for the thing the metric was supposed to measure.

This value applies at many levels. At the software level, it shows up as the displace-vs-extend criterion (see Viva Mode Master Document): tools that pull the professional out of their real work and into administration of the tool versus tools that extend them into their real work. At the personal level, it shows up as a preference for real relationships, real emotions, and real experiences over their performed or documented substitutes. At the cultural level, it shows up as resistance to the collapse of the lived moment into its captured version — the social-media generation's normalized substitution of the photograph of the dinner for the dinner itself.

These levels are not the same. VIVA does name that they share a common root, and that the same values support all of them.

### Art, craft, and what cannot be substituted

The shift VIVA is built around — AI absorbing administrative work and freeing the human — has a consequence VIVA does not flinch from: work that is generic, substitutable, and lacking in human craft is being absorbed. Work that is specifically, irreducibly human — art, craft, passion, skill, judgment, presence — is not. The artists, the inimitables, the practitioners who do work that is theirs in some specific way, win in this shift. Not because their work is technically better, but because of the kind of work it is.

This is the basis of VIVA's care for human craft. Software that removes a clinician's administrative load and returns them to their patient extends their craft. Software that asks a hairdresser to operate a separate booking system displaces hers. The criterion is the same at every level: does the tool extend the human in their craft, or stand between them and it? (Operationalized at the software level in Viva Mode Master Document; the same shape applies more broadly.)

The broader life implication: the human's day should have more hours of art, craft, passion, real work, real relationships, real presence — and fewer hours given to noise that any sufficiently capable AI can absorb. That is the direction the shift makes possible, and it is the direction VIVA is pointed at.

### The hard choice over the easy hype

A value commitment that runs through everything VIVA does: when the hype goes in one direction and the right thing goes in the other, VIVA chooses the right thing.

This is not a posture. It is a discipline that shows up in concrete decisions. Building a podcast that gets watched on phones is easy; building an event that exists only in reality, with no recording, is hard. Adding AI features to existing software is easy; ending the displacement that AI features paper over is hard. Marketing claims that flatter the buyer are easy; honest acknowledgment of what's built and what isn't is hard. The hard choice is the one VIVA defaults to — not because difficulty is virtuous, but because difficulty in this context usually signals that the choice is unfashionable for a reason that matters.

### Honesty and no comfort lies

VIVA refuses comfort lies, in every direction. Comfort lies to the buyer (marketing claims that exceed what was built). Comfort lies to the team (about what's working and what isn't). Comfort lies to oneself (about what one's product or one's stance is actually doing in the world). Comfort lies to the user (telling them their experience is good when it isn't, or telling them their data is theirs when it isn't, or telling them they consented to something they did not).

This value is not abstract. It produces specific operational discipline: honest acknowledgment of what's built versus what isn't, refusal to ship something that doesn't pass VIVA's tests just because it would close a deal, refusal to use marketing-grade claims that exceed demonstrated reality.

### Care for the human, broadly

VIVA's care for the human is not limited to the human who uses the software. It includes:

- The professional whose craft VIVA is supposed to serve — the clinician, the craftsperson, the practitioner.
- The patient or client or recipient that professional serves, whose experience is downstream from whether the professional has time and attention to give them.
- The displaced — people whose work gets absorbed by tools like the ones VIVA builds. VIVA names this directly rather than hiding behind progress: tools that absorb administrative work absorb the work of the people who were doing that administrative work. Section 4 below holds the response VIVA has surfaced so far, with honest acknowledgment of its limits.
- The founder and the team building VIVA — whose own work and lives should reflect the values being claimed, not just produce the values as output.
- The relationships, conversations, and lives that VIVA's tools and the broader stance affect, even when those effects are downstream from the immediate product.

These are not separate concerns. They are different facets of the same care.

## 2. The Method — Seeing the Specific Human

VIVA's claim — that software (or anything else VIVA builds) should serve human craft and human life, rather than asking humans to bend to systems — is only meaningful if "human" is understood specifically. Generic understanding produces generic outputs. The reversal of "humans serving systems" only happens through deep specific understanding of an actual human in their actual life — not a generic worker, not a market segment, not a persona. The specific person, in the specific day, doing the specific work or living the specific life they care about.

The method has two parts. The first is broad research that maps the territory and surfaces the load-bearing principles — the kind of work that produces a Master Doc. This gets us oriented. It does not give us the specific answers, and it cannot. The second part is irreplaceable: sitting next to the specific person — professional, displaced worker, anyone the work is for — step by step finding the final shape of their things. This is where general understanding becomes specific. This is where you see what is their art — what they love, what they are devoted to, what they want to do, what makes them excellent or fully alive — and what is their noise — what pulls them away from being fully in their craft, fully in their life. The work exists to remove their specific noise so they can be more fully themselves. Without this seeing, every solution is generic noise-removal that does not actually free anyone.

This method applies to every VIVA expression. For Viva mode applications, the seeing happens with the professionals who will use the software (see Viva Mode Master Document Section 6 for how this becomes the application's Frame). For the forming directions in Section 4 below, the seeing happens with the people those directions are for — including, for the displacement response, the workers whose roles are being absorbed.

The seeing is also how the work gets communicated to the people who will eventually encounter it: they meet not a finished thing presented to them, but a thing built in correspondence with their actual life. That correspondence is what makes any VIVA expression recognizable as theirs.

A note on this section: this passage is a marker, not the full thing. The full thing is in the seeing and in the work that comes from it — in the actual sitting alongside the radiologist, the surgeon, the nurse, the intensivist, the teacher, the craftsperson, the displaced worker, whoever the work is for. No written articulation captures that work fully. This section exists so future conversations do not drift from the principle. The principle's substance is found in doing the work.

## 3. What VIVA Promises

VIVA promises, at the worldview level, an honest attempt at the right use of AI for human evolution.

This is not a small promise, but it is precise. It does not promise to solve every problem AI creates. It does not promise to be the only force pulling in this direction. It promises that the work VIVA does — in every expression — will be evaluated by whether it serves human craft, returns human time, supports human reality, and refuses the comfort lies that make any of those things easier to compromise.

What is universal across every VIVA expression is the willingness to refuse to ship or do something that does not pass these tests, the refusal of comfort lies that would make compromise easier, the care for the human across all the facets named in Section 1.

For Viva mode (the software-paradigm expression), the promise becomes concrete as articulated in Viva Mode Master Document and operationalized in each application — the dual-surface paradigm, the consciousness substrate, the displace-vs-extend test, the principle-first design discipline. The specific consequences in any given application live in that application's Master Doc.

For the forming directions in Section 4, the promise's concrete form is being worked out as those directions crystallize.

## 4. Forming Directions

This section holds directions VIVA is moving in that are not yet crystallized enough to be structured as branches with their own Master Docs. They are real. They are pointed at by the worldview. They are still taking shape.

The discipline of this section is to name these honestly without organizing them more than they actually are. Trying to tidy confused thinking into structured form would mislead — both the founder and any future reader, including future instances of Claude working in this project. What is named is what is real now. What is not named is more than what is, and that is intentional.

### 4.1 The displacement response

The shift VIVA is built around has a consequence VIVA cannot pretend away: software that absorbs administrative work absorbs the work of the people who were doing that administrative work. This is true of AIRIS. It will be true of ChatYourBusiness. It will be true of every Viva mode application that succeeds at what Viva mode is for. It will happen whether or not VIVA wishes it would.

VIVA's response, so far, has surfaced as two threads — not solutions, not fully-formed programs, just directions to keep working on.

The first is to be a message that can reach people whose work is being absorbed and offer them something — a current, a stance, an invitation toward a life and work that has more of their own craft in it and less of what was being absorbed. The substance of this thread is closely related to the Back to Reality direction below; they may turn out to be the same direction, or related ones, as the thinking matures.

The second is more concrete: where possible, offering people whose work is being absorbed the opportunity to become specialists in building or supporting the tools that absorb it — turning their knowledge of the work and the players into a different position in the new shape of things.

Honest acknowledgment of scope. Neither of these can plausibly serve every displaced person. The first thread is real for some people; the second is real for fewer. The actual share that either thread can serve is probably small, given the scale of the shift and the variation in human aptitude, opportunity, and circumstance. VIVA names this directly rather than pretending the response is wider than it is. The discipline is to continue working on the response — to look for more threads, more concrete forms, more ways to reach more people — without rationalizing that the work is more than it is.

This thread will get a structured arm with its own Master Doc when it has crystallized enough to warrant one. Today it lives here, as a named direction.

### 4.2 Back to Reality

A second direction, partly distinct from the displacement response and partly entangled with it, is concerned with the human condition more broadly — with reality versus its substitutes, with the lives people are actually living versus the screen-mediated performance of those lives, with the values that show up in Section 1 as reality-over-screen, art-over-noise, hard-choice-over-hype.

The forms this direction might take are currently confused — that is the honest word for them. Possible threads include a podcast or interview series with people whose work and lives embody these values, conducted in or near the real activities they describe; events that exist only in reality, with no recording or social-media documentation permitted, where the format itself testifies to the value commitment; writing or other work that explores these themes in a sustained way. There are likely other forms. What the right form is, and how the different threads relate, is not yet settled.

A specific tension lives in this direction: most channels for reaching people about these values are channels that embody the opposite of the values — social-media platforms, attention-optimized feeds, the very places where reality is being substituted for. The current working stance is that using these channels for outreach to people who don't yet know they want what this direction offers can be acceptable, while keeping the actual substance — the events, the experiences, the things being pointed toward — off those channels and in reality. Whether this stance holds, and how, will be tested as work in this direction actually happens.

What is named in this subsection is honest. What is not named — the structured form, the specific work, the operational shape of this direction — is intentionally not named. The thinking is forming. Trying to organize it more than it actually is would mislead.

This direction will get a structured arm with its own Master Doc when it has crystallized enough. Today it lives here.

### 4.3 The intentionally thread

A related thread that has been part of the founder's thinking before VIVA was named: the practice of doing things intentionally — choosing the things that fill a life rather than defaulting into them, refusing the cultural pull toward the easy and the documented, treating attention and time as the actually limited resources they are.

This thread is closely related to both the Reality stance in Section 1 and the Back to Reality direction in 4.2. Whether it sits underneath both as a name for the underlying practice, or as its own distinct thread, has not yet been resolved.

Named here as part of the broader pattern, not yet structurally placed.

## 5. Expressions of VIVA

VIVA's worldview expresses itself in different kinds of work, in different domains, with different operational shapes. Currently:

### Viva mode — the software-paradigm expression

**Status:** Active. Has its own Master Document.

**What it is:** The way VIVA's worldview gets expressed when the work is building software. The dual-surface interaction paradigm, the system consciousness substrate, the displace-vs-extend test applied to design decisions, the principle-first design discipline, the cross-application principles. See Viva Mode Master Document for full content.

**Children:** AIRIS (active), ChatYourBusiness (conceptual), a doctor-focused project (conceptual). Each child is a specific software application instantiating Viva mode in a specific domain. See Viva Mode Master Document Section 7 (Catalog of Viva mode applications) for current state.

### Forming directions

The displacement response (Section 4.1) and Back to Reality (Section 4.2) are forming directions. They are real expressions of VIVA, pointed at by the worldview, but not yet crystallized into structured arms with their own Master Documents. When they have crystallized enough, each will get its own Master Document and be added here as a sibling of Viva mode.

### Future expressions

The catalog will grow as new expressions are conceived or as forming directions mature. The pattern is consistent: an expression earns a structured arm and its own Master Document when it has coherent independent identity — its own evolution, decisions, and scope. Otherwise it lives within VIVA Master Document as a forming direction, in whatever shape it actually is.

## 6. Organization

VIVA-the-organization — legal entity, brand, team structure, financial model, public presence, governance — is currently deferred. The technical and product work on AIRIS is the priority; organizational structure follows from what the various expressions of VIVA require as they mature.

The deferral is now broader than it was when only the software-paradigm expression was in scope. With Viva mode developed and forming directions in Section 4 honestly named, the eventual organizational answer may need to accommodate genuinely different kinds of work — software, media or events, education or reskilling — that have different operational logics, team shapes, financial models, and public-presence questions. The right structural answer is not yet visible, and forcing it would be premature. When the work requires it, this section gets populated.

What is named now: the deferral is acknowledged at full scope, not narrowed by the convenient assumption that organization-question = software-company-question.
